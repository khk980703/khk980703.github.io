Foundation Reflection



1. What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

Through the Human SKills learning, I've discovered that I have a strong sense of initiative, which could potentially help me in the future where leadership or action is needed. I also became more aware of my emotional intelligence, realizing how it plays a key role in how I understand and connect with others. Lastly, I've learnt that anyone including myself are capable of adapting and growing, especially when we embrace a growth mindset.



2. What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values, empathy, and self-awareness play a crucial role in learning and programming by shaping how we interact, solve problems, and grow. Being grounded in values helps guide our decisions and integrity in our work. Empathy allows us to collaborate effectively and create user-centered solutions, while self-awareness helps us recognize our strengths and areas for improvement, empowering us to share ideas confidently and reach our full potential.



3. What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Understanding how neuroplasticity works gives me a powerful sense of control over my own growth. Knowing that I can improve in areas I’ve struggled with shows me what’s possible through effort and persistence.



4. What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

I’ve always found it challenging to introduce myself to strangers. It’s something that used to make me feel uncomfortable or hesitant. However, throughout the Human Skills journey, I’ve noticed a real shift in myself. I’ve become more confident and open, and that initial discomfort is starting to fade as I grow more comfortable engaging with new people.



5. Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because success in web development isn't just about writing good code, it's also about being able to work effectively with others. Collaboration, communication, and emotional intelligence are essential in real-world team environments. By strengthening these skills, we’re better prepared to contribute meaningfully, solve problems together, and thrive in professional settings.



6. Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not at all! I don’t see the time spent on Human Skills as a waste. In fact, I believe it’s a valuable part of the learning experience. Developing these skills has helped me grow not just as a programmer, but as a person. The ability to communicate well, understand others, and reflect on myself will benefit me in any team setting, job interview, or life situation. It complements technical skills and prepares me for real-world challenges beyond just writing code.
