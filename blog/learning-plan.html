Foundation Learning Plan



1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

Working in the IT industry has been my career path for a while. I wish to become a software developer and learn different languages along the way, as I love exploring new things every time. I think I would be satisfied with this career path, as I possess strong tenacity.



2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My strength and limitation are actually closely related. My strength is that when I deeply get into a certain task or topic, I tend to focus really well and with high efficiency.


On the other hand, I may lose focus sometimes, especially when it's something that I'm struggling with. However, I can quickly acknowledge my focus level to take a rest if necessary.


Another strength that I believe I have is strong adaptability. After learning and understanding a concept, I like to think about how I could apply the knowledge in practical use.



3. What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to develop my bravery in human skills. Asking questions or sharing thoughts with others has been somewhat of a weakness for me. I'm used to working in an independent environment, so I would like to overcome the fear of sharing my thoughts with others.



4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I will try to work with my cohort mates, sharing my progress on my workload and maybe getting used to asking silly questions in order to overcome my fear.


If I find myself still struggling, I will contact one of the facilitators, hoping to solve the issue.



5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I think, in order for that to happen smoothly, getting comfortable with my cohort mates and facilitators is crucial. Of course, before sharing my issue, I would do thorough research online for the sake of learning.



6. A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

In the future, if I run into a nasty error that I’m stuck on for a long time, I hope the facilitator could briefly look at my code and point out the specific area I need to focus on to fix the error. That would be extremely helpful in relieving stress.



7. Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

Usually, I have set days and times to go bouldering for my outdoor activities. Other than that, I can’t think of any other fixed activities throughout my week.





Bootcamp Learning Plan



1. What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

As I mentioned earlier, I aspire to become a software developer. I want to strengthen my technical skills while also building my human skills so I can excel in collaboration and communication.



2. What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Throughout my foundation, I believe my strengths have remained consistent and have been extremely beneficial. I’ve also realized that my curiosity about certain concepts can be a real asset during the Bootcamp.



3. What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Since we’ve been working entirely online during the foundation, I’m curious to see how I’ll adapt in an offline enviornment. I hope to stay engaged in my learning and overcome any hesitation, becoming more active and confident in face-to-face interactions.



4. What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I’d like to continue developing my ability to share my ideas more confidently and speak more comfortably in front of others.



5. What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My expectation of the learning environment in Bootcamp is that it will be more focused on self-directed learning, with facilitators available throughout the day to support us. I’m also really looking forward to experiencing pair programming and collaborating with others.



6. What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I will of course arrive at 8:30am at our site. Get ready with my mental attitude in some way that suits me. If I find myself stuck on something for too long, I’ll have a mental limit in place,and once I reach it, I’ll make sure to ask for help rather than stay stuck.

During pair programming, I’ll be open and receptive to my partner’s ideas and approaches. I believe that everyone has a unique way of solving problems, and I’m genuinely interested in learning from different perspectives and exploring alternative solutions.
