Te Whare Tapa Whā



Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori model of health and well-being that likens a wharenui (meeting house) to a person’s overall wellness, with each of its four walls symbolizing a key aspect of well-being: taha tinana (physical health), taha hinengaro (mental and emotional health), taha whānau (family and social connections), and taha wairua (spiritual well-being).



Wall Activities

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

Going to bouldering gym 3 times a week.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

Spending time to comunicate during lunch and dinner while I'm at home.

Going for a walk with my parents once a week.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Spending times with friends online or offline in my free time.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Walking once a week absorbing the nature and the air.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Spend some time while I'm out to appreciate the enviornment.
