Emotional Intelligence



What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence refers to the ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions, both in oneself and others.

It involves key skills such as identifying emotions, evaluating how others feel, controlling one's own emotions, perceiving how others feel, using emotions to facilitate social communication, and relating to others.

EQ can be measured through self-report assessments, where individuals answer questions about their feelings, thoughts, and behaviors. Alternatively, an observer can evaluate someone else's abilities and behaviors.



How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ measures cognitive abilities like problem-solving and knowledge, while EQ focuses on emotional and social skills.

While IQ assesses intellectual capacity, EQ involves understanding and managing emotions, which can be just as crucial for success in life and work.



Why is EQ important?

EQ is crucial because it enhances personal and professional relationships, helps with decision-making, and improves emotional well-being.

People with high EQ are better at managing stress, empathizing with others, and communicating effectively, which contributes to greater success in various aspects of life.
